How Do I Ask for a Donation?

By Email or Online Contact Form

  • Make sure the donor isn't on the "Donors Approached" list.
  • Start your email introduction: use this template to copy into your email.
  • Customize the email for your donor.
  • Attach one of the donation request letters from above.
  • OR link to the letter instead when filling in online forms that cannot include attachments. Links are in the template.
  • Tell us about your contact so we can add it to the donor list. This is important so that the same donor is not bothered multiple times.

By Phone

  • Make sure the donor isn't on the "Donors Approached" list.
  • Introduce yourself: "Hi, I'm with the Lakeshore Elementary PTA, and we're holding our annual fundraising auction on March 11."
  • If they are a new donor: "Could you support us by donating a (gift card/class/product) to our auction?"
  • If they are a past donor: "We're really grateful for your past donation (of whatever they gave, if you know). Could we count on you to repeat your past donation this year?"
  • Other points:
    • Proceeds go toward enrichment classes, community events, and classroom funds.
    • We are one of San Francisco's most diverse public schools, serving about 500 students. Many of our kids come from low-income areas and these enrichment classes provide experiences they may not otherwise have access to.
    • We hope to raise $25,000, or roughly 1/4 of our annual expenses.
    • We are a 501(c)(3) nonprofit and their donation is tax-deductible to the extent of the law.
  • Tell us about your contact so we can add it to the donor list. This is important so that the same donor is not bothered multiple times.

In Person

Same as over the phone, except you can print out our donation request letter for new donors to hand to the store manager.
